Chicken Karaage

Description

Chicken Karaage is a Japanese dish of marinated and fried chicken. The chicken is typically marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic, and ginger, then coated in potato starch and deep-fried until crispy. It is often served with a side of rice and a dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound chicken thighs, boneless and skinless
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon sake
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup potato starch
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Lemon wedges, for garnish
  • Parsley, chopped, for garnish

Steps

  1. Cut the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, garlic, ginger, sake, mirin, and sugar. Add the chicken pieces and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C).
  4. Coat each piece of marinated chicken with potato starch.
  5. Fry the chicken in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes per batch.
  6. Remove the chicken from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  7. Serve the chicken karaage hot with lemon wedges and chopped parsley for garnish.
